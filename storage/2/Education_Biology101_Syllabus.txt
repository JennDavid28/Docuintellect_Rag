Course Syllabus: Introduction to Biology 101
University Curriculum — Fall Semester

Course Overview
This course is designed for undergraduate students enrolled in the Biology degree program. The curriculum follows Common Core-aligned standards and prepares students for standardized testing including SAT and GRE preparation modules.

Learning Outcomes
By the end of this semester, students will be able to demonstrate competency in cellular biology, complete laboratory assignments, and pass the midterm exam with a grade of at least 70%. Assessment rubrics will be distributed during the first lecture.

Weekly Schedule
Week 1: Introduction and syllabus review
Week 2: Cell structure — lecture and textbook reading assignment
Week 3: Genetics — homework assignment due, study guide provided
Week 4: Midterm exam preparation
Week 5: Midterm exam (covers Weeks 1-4)
Week 6-10: Advanced topics in molecular biology
Week 11: Final project presentations
Week 12: Final exam review and semester grade calculation

Grading Policy
Participation: 10%
Homework Assignments: 20%
Midterm Exam: 30%
Final Exam: 40%

Academic Support
Students experiencing difficulty should contact the professor during office hours or enroll in the tutoring program through the Learning Management System (LMS). Special education accommodations (IEP) are coordinated through the Dean of Students office.

Faculty Information
This course is taught by tenured faculty in the Department of Biology. Teaching assistants will hold weekly study sessions to help students prepare for exams and complete assignments. Enrollment is limited to 35 students per classroom section.

Continuing Education Note
This course also satisfies professional development requirements for K-12 teachers seeking certification renewal through our online e-learning platform.
